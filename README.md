# Nigerian Supermarket Sales and Inventory Analysis

**Project Title:** Monthly Inventory Movement & Sales Trend Analysis – Nigerian Supermarket

**Dataset:** Chiamaka Ndubuisi Super Market Dataset (Kaggle)  
**Link:** https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/chiamakandubuisi/super-market-dataset

### Objective
To simulate real-world inventory analysis similar to my role at BON P Superstore (using QuickBooks Enterprise). I analyzed sales and stock movement data from Nigerian supermarket branches in Lagos, Abuja, Ogun, and Port Harcourt to identify trends, top performers, and restocking opportunities.

### Tools Used
- Power BI Desktop (for interactive dashboard)
- Excel (for initial cleaning and validation)
- Kaggle Dataset (Nigerian supermarket sales)

### Key Findings
- **Branch Performance**: Lagos recorded the highest revenue, followed by Port Harcourt and Ogun. Abuja performed moderately, while Unidentified was minimal.
- **Product Performance**: Food & Beverages was the top-selling category by quantity. Electronics and Home Supplies followed. Fashion, Sports Equipment, and Health & Beauty were slower-moving.
- **Sales Trends**: Revenue fluctuated throughout the period with higher values in early months and a decline toward the end (Nov–Dec), suggesting possible seasonal effects.
- **Payment Methods**: Credit Card (33.36%) was the most used, closely followed by Cash (32.82%) and Ewallet (31.35%).
- **Gender Analysis**: Female customers generated significantly higher revenue than Male customers.

### Insights & Recommendations
- Focus marketing and restocking efforts on Food & Beverages and Lagos branch.
- Investigate year-end sales drop for potential promotions or inventory adjustments.
- Encourage Credit Card and Ewallet usage through targeted offers.
- Consider gender-specific promotions since female customers contribute more revenue.
